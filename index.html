Australian Bushfires Data Visualisations

This dashboard explores the patterns and impact of Australian bushfires between 2015 and 2023, along with air pollution levels to see if a relationship exists. The map (left) shows the geographic distribution of fire incidents, while the pie chart (right) highlights the proportions of different fire types per year. Use the slider to explore year-by-year changes.

Now let’s look at state-level data. The scatter plot (left) compares the number of fires and total burned area across states and years, while the stacked bar chart (right) shows how much each state contributes to Australia's total air pollution.

The graphs below show total burned area (left) and average air pollution (right) from 2015–2023. These are later combined to reveal a correlation between the two.

The results confirm a strong relationship between burned area and air pollution—except for 2017, which appears to be an outlier.
